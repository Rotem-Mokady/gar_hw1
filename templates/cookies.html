Cookies: The Secret to Session Persistence

Why Cookies Matter

When a client (your browser) communicates with a server, it does so through a session. A session allows the server to remember you during interactions, but this memory is temporary. Eventually, the server forgets about you, and you may lose your connection.

A cookie is a small piece of data stored by your browser that helps the server remember who you are, ensuring smoother and more efficient interactions.

Cookies are made of key-value pairs that help identify your session, allowing the server to recall information about you across requests.
